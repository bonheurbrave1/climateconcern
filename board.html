Jean Ntazinda

Email: jeanntazinda@climateconcern.rw

Experience:With over fifteen years of experience in the carbon market and climate finance, Mr. NTAZINDA has played a key role in designing numerous emission reduction projects for both compliant and voluntary markets, which have generated millions of CERs and VERs. His expertise also extends to supporting the Rwanda National Country Programme and Framework for National Engagement with the Green Climate Fund. Furthermore, he contributed to the development of the Standardised Crediting Framework, piloted by the World Bank in the Ci-Dev Countries, for the implementation of Article 6 of the Paris Agreement in Rwanda and other African nations. Before establishing Climate Concern in 2012, Mr. NTAZINDA was instrumental in setting up the Designated National Authority (DNA) Secretariat for the carbon market during his time at the Rwanda Environment Management Authority (REMA). Mr. NTAZINDA’s academic and professional journey has consistently focused on GHG mitigation and adaptation to the effects of climate change. He is currently engaged in several consulting assignments at the national and African regional levels, supporting the implementation of Article 6 of the Paris Agreement and the design of emission reduction projects. He holds a BSc in Geography with a focus on Environmental Planning from the National University of Rwanda and a MSc in International Development Administration with a specialization in Climate Change Resilience and Risk Management from Andrews University in Michigan, USA

Omer ELAWAD

Email: omerelawad@climateconcern.rw

Experience:Omer is a climate finance and carbon markets specialist with 18+ years of experience advancing environmental sustainability across Africa. He recently served as a World Bank Consultant (STC) to the Rwanda Environment Management Agency (REMA), spearheading the operationalization of Rwanda’s Standardized Crediting Framework (SCF) and to advance the country’s Article 6 engagement under the Paris Agreement. He provided technical leadership in upgrading Rwanda’s national registry system for A6 compliance, developed the SCF Operations Manual, and facilitated Technical Committee (TC) and Governance Board (GB) meetings to review/approve energy access methodologies (including clean cooking, LPG, mini-grids, and solar systems), protocols, and MRV templates. His work ensured end-to-end SCF functionality—from program listings and issuance requests to institutional governance—while delivering targeted capacity building for REMA’s team on carbon crediting and A6 transactions. As a UNFCCC registered Roster of Experts (ROE) reviewer for GHG inventories and BURs, Omer possesses deep technical credentials in climate policy implementation. He participated as team member to the Sudan’s NDCs and Third National Communication, leading IPPU sector analysis and mitigation strategies, and led Sudan’s first Gold Standard-certified carbon project (fuel-efficient stoves), generating VERs for voluntary markets. His expertise spans compliance and voluntary mechanisms, including advising the Eastern Africa Alliance on Carbon Markets and Climate Finance (EAA) on Article 6 implementation and representing Sudan in regional carbon market dialogues. Omer’s cross-cutting experience includes managing corporate GHG protocols (Haggar Group), leading GCF readiness programs, designing REDD+ frameworks, and mobilizing private climate investments. A Certified Sustainability/CSR Practitioner (CSE) and Chartered Management Institute (CMI UK) member, he has secured funding from World Bank, GCF, and Ci-Dev for projects in 10+ countries, consistently bridging policy, finance, and field implementation to scale climate action.
