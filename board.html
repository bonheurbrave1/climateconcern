Jean Ntazinda

Email: jeanntazinda@climateconcern.rw

Experience:Jean NTAZINDA holds a bachelor degree in geography with major in environmental planning from National University of Rwanda (ur.ac.rw), did graduate studies in International Development Administration at Andrews University-Michigan, USA (andrews.edu) and has post-graduate Climate Change Diplomacy from Diplo-Foundation-Malta (diplomacy.edu). He is career wise equipped with capacity in project design and management, stakeholders’ engagement, matching business development and government programs. Prior to starting Climate Concern Ltd in 2012, Jean NTAZINDA worked for Rwanda Environment Management Authority for 2.5 years as Project manager of a capacity building project for Clean Development Mechanism (CDM) under Kyoto Protocol. Before taking role of a country director for 2 years at delAgua.org in 2013, he participated in design and implementation of its UNFCCC registered CDM programme of activities (PoA) between May 2012-May 2013. From 2016 to date Jean spends his time on consulting work in environment related projects with much focus on climate change adaptation. In the most recent years, he worked on development Climate Change projects supported by GCF, UNDP, GEF and World Bank. Jean NTAZINDA has a good understanding of legal framework and institutional arrangement of environment and climate change at national and international level.

Dominique Kayigire

Email: dominiquekiyigire@climateconcern.rw

Experience:Dominique KAYIGIRE is a partner and Natural Resources consultant at Climate Concern Ltd. He holds an MSc in Geo-Science for Environment and Sustainable Development from the University of Rwanda. Dominique is a co-designer of EnviroFlag, a Climate Concern Ltd program that supports projects and businesses to comply with their environmental commitments in the course of their operations. Before devoting his full time to EnviroFlag and Climate Concern Ltd, Dominique occupied a number of positions in the sphere of natural resources governance in public, private and intergovernmental institutions for the past nine years. He served as the Rwanda Country Manager at RCS Global Group, an internationally credited in the audits of supply chains linking minerals, metal producers and refiners. Rwandan registered and practicing environmental consultant, Dominique worked as the Coordinator of mineral certification in then Rwanda Ministry of Natural Resources. With funds mostly from the EU and USAID, he technically contributed much at policy-making level for the introduction of the Regional Certification Mechanism for minerals in many of the 12 ICGLR member states.
