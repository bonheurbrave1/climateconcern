Our National Scheme Development Approach

Climate Concern specializes in developing comprehensive national climate schemes that align with international standards while addressing country-specific needs and priorities. Our expertise spans carbon markets, climate finance mechanisms, and integrated policy frameworks.

We work closely with governments, institutions, and stakeholders to design robust, scalable, and sustainable national schemes that drive climate action, economic development, and environmental integrity across all sectors.

National Scheme Development Process

A comprehensive approach to designing effective national climate frameworks

1

Needs Assessment & Visioning

Comprehensive analysis of national circumstances, priorities, and stakeholder requirements to define scheme objectives.

2

Stakeholder Engagement

Multi-stakeholder consultations with government, private sector, civil society, and international partners.

3

Framework Design

Development of institutional, legal, and operational frameworks tailored to national context and objectives.

4

Pilot Implementation

Testing scheme components through pilot programs to validate design and identify improvement areas.

5

System Development

Building operational systems, tools, and platforms required for scheme implementation and management.

6

Launch & Scale-up

Full scheme launch with ongoing support for scaling, monitoring, and continuous improvement.

Typical Development Timeline

Standard phases and timeframes for national scheme development

Phase 1: Preparation & Planning (3-6 months)

Stakeholder mapping, needs assessment, baseline studies, and preliminary framework design.

Phase 2: Design & Development (6-12 months)

Detailed framework development, institutional design, legal drafting, and system specifications.

Phase 3: Validation & Testing (3-6 months)

Stakeholder validation, pilot testing, system development, and refinement of frameworks.

Phase 4: Implementation & Launch (6-12 months)

Legal enactment, system deployment, capacity building, and scheme operationalization.

Phase 5: Monitoring & Enhancement (Ongoing)

Performance monitoring, continuous improvement, and scheme evolution based on experience.
